Money-Saving Advice

8 Budget Tips That
Actually Save Money

Practical strategies from real wedding planning experience — not generic advice you've already read.

01

Set Your Total Before Anything Else

Agree on your maximum spend before looking at a single venue, dress, or florist. Every couple who skips this step overspends by 20–40%.

02

Venue Eats Half Your Budget — Plan Accordingly

Lock in your venue cost first, then allocate the rest. Never fall in love with a venue before knowing whether it fits your budget.

03

Never Cut Photography

Photos and video are the only things that last forever. Allocate 10–15% here before any other category. You won't regret it.

04

Get Three Quotes Per Vendor

Prices vary by 40–60% between vendors for identical quality. Always compare three quotes — this single habit saves couples £1,000–3,000.

05

Off-Peak = 20–30% Savings

Friday and Sunday weddings save significantly on venue costs. January, February, and November are typically the cheapest months.

06

Buy Welcome Bag Items in Bulk on Amazon

Snacks, water, comfort items — buying in bulk saves 40–60% versus wedding boutiques. A full welcome bag for each guest for £15–20 is achievable.

07

Use Canva for Stationery

A professionally designed invitation suite printed locally costs £30–60. The same thing from a stationery studio costs £300–600+.

08

Always Keep a 10% Buffer

Leave 10% of your total budget unallocated until your wedding day. Something unexpected always happens — this keeps you calm when it does.
